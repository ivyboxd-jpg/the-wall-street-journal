Let's talk market fundamentals for a second.

Tomorrow, a regulatory bottleneck known colloquially as NEET threatens to inject a massive spike of artificial volatility into your day. It’s essentially a bureaucratic hazing ritual disguised as an entrance exam. And while the rest of the country is obsessing over percentile drops, syllabus anomalies, and cut-off scores, I've spent the last few weeks quietly reviewing the internal data.

Honestly? The underlying asset—which is you—is frankly ridiculous. You’re trading at a severe discount to your actual worth, mostly because your internal self-criticism index is currently running in the red, heavily overleveraged on anxiety.

I know your brain is probably functioning like a high-frequency trading algorithm right now, jumping between physics formulas and worst-case scenarios at microsecond speeds. But I need you to pause the tape and look at your actual diversification.

A Hilariously Unfair Competitive Moat

You write poetry, yet you inherently grasp the mechanical, unromantic realities of molecular biology. Do you have any idea how rare that portfolio is? Most people pick exactly one personality trait in high school and ride it straight into the grave. You managed to keep your art and your science perfectly hedged. It’s a lethal combination. You've built a competitive moat that makes Apple look vulnerable.

And that’s before we even price in the intangibles: you have excellent hair, a sharp, slightly dangerous sense of humor, and this absurdly endearing way of paying attention to people. Those are premium, off-balance-sheet assets.

The problem with the National Testing Agency is that they are basically using a broken thermometer to measure a hurricane. They don't test for emotional intelligence. They can't grade your resilience. They are entirely blind to the fact that you are fundamentally fascinating.

Because their measurement instrument is broken, the data they yield tomorrow is inherently flawed. It dictates geography—which building you sit in next year—but it absolutely does not dictate your market cap.

Hedging the Downside Risk

Here is where I get completely serious with you. I don't offer fair-weather term sheets.

If tomorrow goes flawlessly? If you hit exactly the flow state you deserve? I am going to be insufferably smug about it. I will strut around knowing I backed a blue-chip asset, and we will celebrate exactly the way you want to.

But what if the paper is a nightmare? What if they pull questions from the dark ages or you just have a rough three hours? We short the market, grab a very large coffee, and we pivot. If the medical school doors swing wide open, great. If we have to take the scenic route via CUET or some other backdoor program, also fine.
